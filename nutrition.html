A Complete 4-Week Beginner's Workout/Nutrition Program

Pay Attention

You Might Be Suprised How?

Many active adults overlook the importance of nutrition basics --- and then run short on key nutrients. Not getting enough vitamins, mineral and other nutrients can compromise your health and your performance.

Fuel Up

Even if your goal is to lose weight

Give your body the energy it needs Yet fueling up for activity is as easy as following the well-established rules of a healthy diet: Eat plenty of fruits and vegetables, consume lean proteins, eat healthy fats, get your whole-grain carbohydrates, and drink plenty of fluids, especially water.

Love Carbs

You need them

Rebuild with protein and don't ignore fats be sure to pick unsaturated fats. Good sources are avocado, olive and canola oils, flaxseed and nuts. Know what you need pre-workout. If you work out less than an hour at a time, eating throughout the day should give you enough energy. However, to avoid GI issues, you may want to avoid eating right before you exercise. As a general rule, eat one to three hours before your workout, even if you are going to do sustained, high-intensity activity, like a half marathon. Remember the post-workout your body uses its stored energy sources during a workout. After you exercise, you need to restore those nutrients as soon as possible.
